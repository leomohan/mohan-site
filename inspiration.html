Inspiration

The thinkers, ideas, and experiences that shaped my work and worldview.

On Writing

Why I Write

The decision to write — and to keep writing, across 263+ books — was never about fame or fortune. It was about the discipline of thought. Writing forces clarity. When I write about a technology, I understand it more deeply than if I simply use it.

Stephen King's philosophy — that writing is telepathy, a direct transfer of thought from one mind to another across time and space — resonates deeply. A book I wrote three years ago is still talking to a reader today. That's a kind of immortality that no job title can provide.

"The scariest moment is always just before you start. After that, things can only get better." — Stephen King

I also draw inspiration from the Tamil literary tradition. The Thirukkural — 1,330 couplets written by Thiruvalluvar over two millennia ago — packs more wisdom per word than most modern management books. I return to it often.

On Technology

Staying at the Edge

In cybersecurity, stagnation is a vulnerability. The threat landscape evolves daily; so must the defender. My inspiration comes from practitioners who go deep — who read the actual papers, not just the vendor summaries; who build the labs, not just watch the demos.

Bruce Schneier's thinking on security as a mindset rather than a product has shaped how I advise clients. Security isn't a feature you bolt on; it's a way of designing and thinking. That philosophy runs through everything I write about AI governance and cybersecurity architecture.

The rapid rise of large language models and agentic AI has reinvigorated my intellectual curiosity. Anthropic's work on AI safety — and the Claude model family I am helping bring to MEA organisations — represents the most interesting convergence of technology and ethics I've encountered in my career.

On Leadership

People Before Processes

After 30+ years managing teams, building companies, and advising boards, my core leadership philosophy is simple: invest in people first, and the results follow.

Peter Drucker remains the most practically useful management thinker I have encountered. His observation that culture eats strategy for breakfast is something I see validated every year in the organisations I work with.

"Management is doing things right; leadership is doing the right things." — Peter Drucker

On Life

Curiosity as a Way of Life

My parents instilled in me a simple belief: the day you stop learning is the day you start declining. That belief has kept me studying, writing, and exploring long after most people have settled into comfortable expertise.

Travel has been one of my greatest teachers. Every country I visit offers a different lens on familiar problems. The way a culture organises its cities, its data, its authority — all of it is data about human nature and organisational design.
