Inspiration

The thinkers, ideas, and experiences that shaped my work and worldview.

On Writing

Why I Write

The decision to write — and to keep writing, across 263+ books — was never about fame or fortune. It was about the discipline of thought. Writing forces clarity.

Stephen King’s philosophy — that writing is telepathy, a direct transfer of thought from one mind to another across time and space — resonates deeply.

“The scariest moment is always just before you start. After that, things can only get better.” — Stephen King

I also draw inspiration from the Tamil literary tradition. The Thirukkural packs more wisdom per word than most modern management books. I return to it often.

On Technology

Staying at the Edge

In cybersecurity, stagnation is a vulnerability. Bruce Schneier’s thinking on security as a mindset rather than a product has shaped how I advise clients.

Anthropic’s work on AI safety — and the Claude model family I am helping bring to MEA organisations — represents the most interesting convergence of technology and ethics I’ve encountered in my career.

On Leadership

People Before Processes

Peter Drucker remains the most practically useful management thinker I have encountered.

“Management is doing things right; leadership is doing the right things.” — Peter Drucker

On Life

Curiosity as a Way of Life

My parents instilled a simple belief: the day you stop learning is the day you start declining. Travel has been one of my greatest teachers — every country offers a different lens on familiar problems.
