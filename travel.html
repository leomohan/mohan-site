Travel

Camera in hand, notebook open — exploring the world one country at a time.

Travel is not a hobby for me — it’s research. Every country I visit offers new data about how humans organise themselves, how technology is adopted differently across cultures, and what problems remain universal.

Travel as a Teacher

The best professional development I’ve done happened in conversations with strangers in airports, observing how different societies handle risk, privacy, and technology adoption.

Dubai has been my base for over a decade — a city that moves at the speed of ambition, at the intersection of East and West.

My camera captures the visual; my notebooks capture the stories. Both feed back into everything I write.
