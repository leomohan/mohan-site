Travel

Camera in hand, notebook open — exploring the world one country at a time.

Travel is not a hobby for me — it's research. Every country I visit offers new data about how humans organise themselves, how technology is adopted differently across cultures, and what problems remain universal. Based in Dubai, I have the privilege of being at one of the world's great crossroads, and I use it.

Travel as a Teacher

The best professional development I have ever done didn't happen in a classroom or on a certification platform — it happened in conversations with strangers in airports, in observing how different societies handle risk, privacy, and technology adoption.

Dubai has been my base for over a decade, and it remains one of the most stimulating places on earth to be in technology — a city that moves at the speed of ambition, at the intersection of East and West. But the perspective you gain from leaving regularly, and returning with fresh eyes, is irreplaceable.

My camera captures the visual; my notebooks capture the stories. Both feed back into everything I write.
